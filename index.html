Teaching Examples

Introductory GIS

I teach the Introductory GIS course at Western Washington University. In this course, I focus on the foundational principles and tools needed to be successful navigating Arcgis Pro and Online.

There is a lot of material to cover, so I focus on basic ArcGIS Pro tools, Online mapping, including Field Maps and StoryMaps, geographic projections, and simple ModelBuilder flows.

Cartography and Data Visualization

As the lead instructor for Cartography and Data Visualization at Western Washington University, I have the responsibility and pleasure of teaching our GIS students about proper cartographic conventions and standards, how to communicate science data using maps, and how to have fun with map-making! Additionally, I am responsible for the curriculum and labs used for our GIS courses.

I teach cartography during the Fall and Spring, with the occasional Summer quarter. These students are mainly in the certificate or minor program at WWU.
